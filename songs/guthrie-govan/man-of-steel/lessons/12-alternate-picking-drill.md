# Lesson 12: Alternate Picking Drill
**Phase:** 3 — Speed
**Prerequisites:** Lessons 07-11 (Phase 2 complete)
**Estimated time:** 25 minutes
**You'll need:** Guitar, metronome, tuner

## Goal
Alternate pick 16th notes cleanly at 100bpm on a single string and 80bpm across 3+ strings in D minor.

## Warm-Up (5 min)
Open string alternate picking. Set metronome to 60bpm, 16th notes (4 picks per click). Play down-up-down-up on the low E string for 8 beats, then move to A, D, G, B, high E. Focus entirely on the pick staying close to the strings — no big motions. The pick should barely leave the string surface.

## Concept
**Strict alternate picking** means down-up-down-up continuously, regardless of string changes. You never "reset" to a downstroke when crossing to a new string. The pick motion must be small and relaxed. Tension kills speed — if your hand tightens, you've hit your actual limit and need to back off. Sections C and D of Man of Steel require this for chromatic and scalar runs at high tempo.

## Steps

### Step 1: Single String — Building the Engine
- **Do this:** On the high E string, play frets 12-13-14-15-14-13-12-13 in a repeating loop. Strict alternate picking at 60bpm, 16th notes. Play for 1 full minute without stopping.
- **Listen for:** Every note should be the same volume and clarity. No accent on downstrokes vs upstrokes.
- **Watch for:** ZERO tension in the picking hand. If you feel your hand tightening, stop immediately, shake it out for 10 seconds, and restart. Tension is the enemy — catch it early.
- **Repeat:** 3 times through (3 minutes total, with breaks as needed).

### Step 2: Two Strings — The String Change
- **Do this:** Play high E (12-13-14-15) then B (12-13-14-15), repeating. Strict alternate picking at 60bpm, 16th notes. The string change is where alternate picking gets tricky. If you end on an upstroke on the E string, your next stroke on the B string must be a downstroke (outside picking). Do NOT cheat by adding an extra stroke or restarting with a downstroke.
- **Listen for:** The string change should be seamless — no gap, no accent, no rhythmic hiccup.
- **Repeat:** Until the string change feels as natural as staying on one string (at least 2 minutes).

### Step 3: Three Strings — Expanding Range
- **Do this:** Add the G string: high E (12-13-14-15), B (12-13-14-15), G (12-13-14-15), then back up: B (12-13-14-15), E (12-13-14-15). Start at 60bpm 16th notes.
- **Listen for:** Even rhythm across all string changes. No stumble on the G-to-B or B-to-E transitions.
- **Repeat:** Once comfortable at 60bpm, increase by 5bpm increments. Target 80bpm. Spend at least 30 seconds at each tempo before increasing.

### Step 4: D Minor Scale Across 3 Strings
- **Do this:** Play the D minor scale fragment: E string (13-15-17), B string (13-15-17), G string (14-16-17). Ascending, then descending. Strict alternate picking at 60bpm, 16th notes.
- **Listen for:** The wider fret stretches (13 to 15 to 17) should not cause the picking hand to tense up. Each note rings clearly with no fret buzz.
- **Repeat:** Increase to 70bpm target. If 70 feels solid, try 75.

### Step 5: Chromatic Exercise — Section C Patterns
- **Do this:** On the E string, play frets 8-9-10-11-12. Then on the B string, 8-9-10-11-12. Strict alternate picking. This 5-note-per-string pattern mimics the chromatic runs in Section C (bars 17-24). Start at 60bpm.
- **Listen for:** With 5 notes per string, you alternate which stroke starts each new string (down on E, up on B, down on E...). This is the exact mechanic needed for bars 17-24.
- **Repeat:** Increase tempo in 5bpm increments. Note where it starts to break down.

### Step 6: Find Your Max Clean Speed
- **Do this:** Go back to the single-string exercise from Step 1 (frets 12-13-14-15-14-13-12-13). Increase tempo in 5bpm increments until you can no longer play every note cleanly with zero tension. The last tempo where it was clean is your current max.
- **Write it down.** This is your baseline for the coming lessons. You'll track progress against this number.

## Checkpoint
- [ ] Can you alternate pick cleanly at 100bpm (16th notes) on a single string?
- [ ] Can you alternate pick cleanly at 80bpm across 3 strings with no rhythmic hiccups at string changes?
- [ ] Is your picking hand relaxed at target tempo — no white knuckles, no forearm tension?
- [ ] Have you noted your max clean speed baseline?

**All checked?** → Move to Lesson 13
**Not yet?** → Repeat this lesson next session. Focus on the step that broke down.
