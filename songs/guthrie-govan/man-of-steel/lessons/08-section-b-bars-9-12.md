# Lesson 08: Section B — Bars 9-12
**Phase:** 2 — Legato & Dynamics
**Prerequisites:** Lesson 07
**Estimated time:** 25 minutes
**You'll need:** Guitar, metronome, tuner

## Goal
Play bars 9-12 of Man of Steel cleanly at 50% tempo with smooth legato phrasing and audible position shifts via slides.

## Context
This lesson begins learning Section B of the solo (bars 9-16). The solo is in D minor, standard tuning, approximately 56 bars total. By now you have completed Phase 1 (vibrato, bending, Section A bars 1-8, Section F bars 49-56 at 50-60% tempo) and Lesson 07 (legato fundamentals).

Bars 9-12 transition from the melodic opening of Section A into more active playing. You will encounter legato passages primarily on the B and high E strings around frets 14-19, with position shifts accomplished through slides. This is where the solo starts to build momentum.

Reference: See your tab.txt Section B or PDF page 1 bottom / page 2 top.

## Warm-Up (5 min)
Run the D natural minor legato pattern from Lesson 07: High E string (13-15-17), B string (13-15-17), G string (14-16-17) — ascending and descending. Use a metronome at 60-70bpm, 16th notes. Do this for 5 minutes to get your legato hand warmed up and your fingers stretched for the fret 14-19 range.

## Steps

### Step 1: Learn bars 9-10 note by note
- **Do this:** Open your tab for bars 9-10. Play through each note individually at an extremely slow tempo — no metronome yet. Identify every note and its technique marking: which notes are picked, which are hammer-ons (h), pull-offs (p), and slides (s). Write a mental map or mark your tab: "pick here, hammer here, slide here."
- **Listen for:** Each note ringing clearly on its own. Do not worry about speed or flow yet. Just get the notes right.
- **Repeat:** Play through bars 9-10 at least 5 times very slowly until you can play every note without stopping to look up the tab.

### Step 2: Nail the slides
- **Do this:** Find every slide marked in bars 9-10. These are how Guthrie shifts position on the neck — for example, sliding from fret 15 to fret 17 to move up. Practice each slide in isolation. Pick the starting note, then slide to the target fret with even pressure.
- **Listen for:** The slide should be audible (you hear the pitch glide) but not accented — it should not be louder than the notes around it. The arrival note should ring at the same volume as the departure note. If the string goes dead during the slide, you are releasing pressure. Keep your finger firmly on the string throughout.
- **Repeat:** Each slide 5 times in isolation, then put them back in context.

### Step 3: Learn bars 11-12
- **Do this:** Same process as Step 1 but for bars 11-12. These bars get slightly busier with more legato notes per beat. Take them note by note. Identify all technique markings.
- **Listen for:** Bars 11-12 have more hammer-ons and pull-offs in sequence. Apply what you learned in Lesson 07 — keep the volume even between picked and legato notes.
- **Repeat:** 5 times very slowly until memorized.

### Step 4: Connect bars 9-12 as one phrase
- **Do this:** Play all four bars in sequence without stopping. Still no metronome — just a slow, steady pace. The goal is to feel bars 9-12 as one continuous musical phrase, not four separate bars. Let the notes flow into each other.
- **Listen for:** Any spot where you hesitate or stumble. That is the spot that needs isolated practice. Loop just the two beats around the problem area 10 times, then put it back into the full 4-bar phrase.
- **Repeat:** Until you can play bars 9-12 three times through without any stops or stumbles.

### Step 5: Add dynamics
- **Do this:** Bar 9 starts where Section A left off — moderate intensity. Through bars 10-12, build the volume and energy slightly. This is not a dramatic crescendo, just a gradual increase in intensity. Think of it as the solo leaning forward.
- **Listen for:** A subtle but audible difference in energy between bar 9 and bar 12. Bar 12 should feel more driving and urgent than bar 9.
- **Repeat:** 3 times, focusing only on the dynamic shape.

### Step 6: Bring up to tempo with metronome
- **Do this:** Set your metronome to 40% of the original tempo. Play bars 9-12 three times cleanly. Then increase to 45%, then 50%. If you cannot get three clean passes at a tempo, stay there. Do not skip ahead.
- **Listen for:** Rhythmic accuracy — every note landing where it should relative to the beat. Legato notes evenly spaced. Slides timed correctly (not too fast, not dragging).
- **Repeat:** Spend 5-8 minutes working from 40% up to 50%.

## Checkpoint
- [ ] Can you play bars 9-12 cleanly at 50% tempo?
- [ ] Are the legato notes (hammer-ons, pull-offs) even in volume with the picked notes?
- [ ] Are position shifts via slides smooth and audible but not accented?
- [ ] Can you connect bars 7-12 (end of Section A into Section B) without a gap? Try it once to check.
