# Lesson 09: Section B — Bars 13-16 (Section B Complete)
**Phase:** 2 — Legato & Dynamics
**Prerequisites:** Lesson 08
**Estimated time:** 25 minutes
**You'll need:** Guitar, metronome, tuner

## Goal
Play the full Section B (bars 9-16) at 55% tempo and connect it with Section A so that you can play bars 1-16 as one continuous piece at 50% tempo.

## Context
This lesson completes Section B of the Man of Steel solo (D minor, standard tuning, ~56 bars). You already know bars 9-12 from Lesson 08. Bars 13-16 raise the intensity further — wider intervals, faster note groupings, and more demanding legato sequences. This is the section where the solo shifts from melodic storytelling into something more physically intense, setting up the shredding that comes in Section C.

By the end of this lesson, you will have the entire first third of the solo (bars 1-16) under your fingers.

Reference: See your tab.txt Section B or PDF page 2.

## Warm-Up (5 min)
Play bars 9-12 from Lesson 08 twice through at 50% tempo with your metronome. This serves as both warm-up and review. If bars 9-12 feel rusty, spend an extra minute on any rough spots before moving on.

## Steps

### Step 1: Learn bars 13-14 slowly
- **Do this:** Open your tab for bars 13-14. Play through note by note at very slow tempo, no metronome. These bars increase intensity — expect wider interval jumps that may require position shifts. Identify every technique marking (h, p, s, bends). Map out where your hand needs to be for each beat.
- **Listen for:** Any position shift that catches you off guard. If there is a jump of 4+ frets, figure out whether Guthrie uses a slide, a shift, or a stretch to get there. Practice the shift in isolation.
- **Repeat:** 5 times through very slowly until memorized.

### Step 2: Learn bars 15-16
- **Do this:** Same process for bars 15-16. These are the peak of Section B — the most intense legato work before Section C begins. Expect the fastest note groupings you have encountered so far. If any passage feels impossible at tempo, do not panic — you only need it at 55% right now.
- **Listen for:** Clarity in the fast passages. Even at slow tempo, every note must speak. If notes are blurring together, you are going too fast. Slow down further.
- **Repeat:** 5 times through very slowly.

### Step 3: Connect bars 13-16
- **Do this:** Play bars 13-16 as one phrase. No metronome yet, just a steady slow pace. Find the seams — the spots between bars where you stumble. Isolate each seam (the last beat of one bar into the first beat of the next) and loop it 10 times.
- **Listen for:** Smooth connections between bars. The phrase should feel like it builds continuously through all four bars, not like four separate chunks.
- **Repeat:** Until three clean passes without stops.

### Step 4: Full Section B with metronome
- **Do this:** Set metronome to 40% tempo. Play all of Section B: bars 9-16. This is the first time you are playing all eight bars together. Expect the transitions from bars 12 to 13 to be the weakest link — that is where old material meets new material.
- **Listen for:** Consistent tempo throughout. Do not slow down in the harder bars (13-16) and speed up in the easier ones (9-12). The metronome keeps you honest.
- **Repeat:** Increase by 5% increments: 40% → 45% → 50% → 55%. Three clean passes at each tempo before moving up.

### Step 5: Connect Sections A and B (bars 1-16)
- **Do this:** Now play from the very beginning of the solo. Bars 1-8 (Section A, which you learned in Phase 1) straight into bars 9-16 (Section B). Set the metronome to 50% tempo. This is the first big chunk of the solo — roughly the first third.
- **Listen for:** The transition from Section A into Section B. Section A is melodic and expressive (vibrato, bends). Section B is more legato and forward-moving. The shift in character should be audible but not jarring — like a storyteller who starts speaking a little faster and more urgently.
- **Repeat:** 3 times through.

### Step 6: Identify weak spots for next session
- **Do this:** Play bars 1-16 one final time at 50%. As you play, mentally note any bar or transition that feels shaky. These are your priority spots for review in future sessions.
- **Listen for:** The overall dynamic arc. Bar 1 should be the most restrained. Bar 16 should be the most intense. There should be a clear sense of building energy across the 16 bars.
- **Repeat:** Once, with full concentration.

## Checkpoint
- [ ] Can you play full Section B (bars 9-16) cleanly at 55% tempo?
- [ ] Can you play Sections A + B connected (bars 1-16) at 50% tempo without stopping?
- [ ] Is there a smooth dynamic build from the melodic opening (bar 1) to the intense close of Section B (bar 16)?

**All checked?** → Move to Lesson 10
**Not yet?** → Repeat this lesson next session. Focus on whichever checkpoint isn't passing.
