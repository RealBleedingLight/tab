# Lesson 13: Section C — Bars 17-20
**Phase:** 3 — Speed
**Prerequisites:** Lesson 12 (Alternate Picking Drill)
**Estimated time:** 30 minutes
**You'll need:** Guitar, metronome, tuner

## Goal
Play bars 17-20 of Man of Steel at 45% tempo (~54bpm if full tempo is 120bpm) with clean alternate picking and accurate chromatic passages.

## Context
Section C (bars 17-24) is where the shredding starts. The solo shifts from the legato lines of Section B into fast scalar and chromatic runs across multiple strings, often grouped as sextuplets (6 notes per beat). This is the most technically demanding material in the solo alongside Section D. You are learning bars 17-20 today and will complete Section C in Lesson 14.

**Reference:** See tab.txt Section C or the PDF (page 1 bottom / page 2 top). Have the tab open during this lesson.

## Warm-Up (5 min)
Chromatic alternate picking drill from Lesson 12: on the E string play frets 8-9-10-11-12, then B string 8-9-10-11-12. Strict alternate picking at 70bpm, 16th notes. Run through this for 5 minutes to get the picking hand warm and loose.

## Steps

### Step 1: Learn Bars 17-18 Note by Note
- **Do this:** With the tab open, learn bars 17-18 one note at a time. Do NOT use a metronome yet. Just find every note on the fretboard and play through the passage slowly enough that you never play a wrong note.
- **Listen for:** Chromatic runs — patterns like 8-10-11-12-11-10-9-8. Identify the underlying pattern. Fast passages are almost always built from a short repeating cell (4-6 notes) that shifts position. Find the cell and learn the PATTERN, not just individual notes.
- **Repeat:** Play through bars 17-18 at least 5 times with no metronome until the notes are memorized.

### Step 2: Isolate the Hardest Passage in Bars 17-18
- **Do this:** Identify the single hardest bar or half-bar in bars 17-18. This is usually where the most string changes or position shifts happen. Loop JUST that fragment at 30% tempo with a metronome.
- **Listen for:** Every note clean, every pick stroke correct (strict alternate). No rhythmic rushing or dragging.
- **Repeat:** Gradually increase to 40% tempo. Spend at least 5 minutes on this fragment alone.

### Step 3: Learn Bars 19-20 Slowly
- **Do this:** Same approach as Step 1 — learn bars 19-20 note by note, no metronome first. These bars likely continue the scalar intensity and may feature sextuplet groupings (6 notes per beat).
- **Listen for:** Identify whether the rhythm is 16th notes (4 per beat) or sextuplets (6 per beat). This changes the feel significantly.
- **Repeat:** 5 times through without metronome.

### Step 4: Sextuplet Rhythm Training
- **Do this:** If bars 19-20 contain sextuplets, practice the rhythm on a single note first. Set metronome to 40% tempo. Play 6 evenly spaced notes per click on the open E string. Count "1-e-and-a-and-a" or simply feel groups of 6. Once the rhythm is locked in, add the left-hand fingering from the tab.
- **Listen for:** All 6 notes should be perfectly even — no "gallop" feel, no rushing the last 2 notes.
- **Repeat:** Until the sextuplet rhythm feels natural (at least 1 minute on open string, then add notes).

### Step 5: Connect Bars 17-20
- **Do this:** Play bars 17-20 continuously at 35% tempo with metronome. This is the first time you're stringing all 4 bars together. It will feel slow compared to the recording — that's correct. Speed comes from accuracy, not from pushing.
- **Listen for:** Smooth transitions between bars. No pauses at bar lines. Every note and rhythm accurate.
- **Repeat:** Once solid at 35%, increase to 40%, then 45%. Do not jump tempos — move in 5% increments.

### Step 6: Three Clean Passes
- **Do this:** Play bars 17-20 three times at 45% tempo with metronome. Between each pass, pause for 10 seconds and shake out your hands. After each pass, mentally note which bars or beats broke down.
- **Listen for:** Consistency across all three passes. If the same spot breaks every time, that spot needs isolated practice next session.
- **Repeat:** If you can't get 3 clean passes at 45%, drop to 40% and do 3 clean passes there instead.

## Checkpoint
- [ ] Can you play bars 17-20 at 45% tempo with metronome?
- [ ] Are chromatic passages accurate — no wrong notes, no skipped notes?
- [ ] Is the sextuplet rhythm even (if applicable)?
- [ ] Is the picking hand relaxed throughout?

**All checked?** → Move to Lesson 14
**Not yet?** → Repeat this lesson next session. Focus on the specific bars that broke down.
