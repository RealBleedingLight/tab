# Lesson 03: Opening Theme — Bars 1-4
**Phase:** 1 — Foundation
**Prerequisites:** Lessons 01, 02
**Estimated time:** 25 minutes
**You'll need:** Guitar, metronome, tuner

## Goal
Play bars 1-4 of the Man of Steel solo cleanly at 50% tempo with proper vibrato on sustained notes, accurate bends, and vocal-style phrasing.

## Warm-Up (5 min)
Spend 2 minutes on vibrato: B string fret 15, delayed entry on beat 2, 2 cycles per beat at 60bpm (from Lesson 01). Then spend 3 minutes on bends: 5 full step bends on B string fret 15 (checking accuracy against fret 17), followed by 5 bend-and-vibrato reps where you hold the bend for 3 beats with vibrato (from Lesson 02). Your wrist should feel loose and warm before tackling the solo material.

## Concept: Phrasing
The opening of Man of Steel is not a technical showcase — it is a melody. Guthrie plays it like a singer, not a shredder. Phrasing means shaping a line of notes the way a vocalist would: some notes are emphasized, some are soft, there are natural places to "breathe" (small pauses), and sustained notes get vibrato the way a singer adds warmth to a held note. As you learn bars 1-4, always ask yourself: "If I were singing this, how would it sound?" That instinct will guide your dynamics and timing better than any written instruction.

## Steps

### Step 1: Learn the Notes — Bars 1-2
Open your tab (tab.txt Section A or PDF page 1, first system). Read through the notes of bars 1-2.
- **Do this:** Play each note one at a time, very slowly — no metronome yet. Just find the right frets and strings. The opening lives primarily on the B string and high E string around frets 13-17 in the D minor area. Play through bars 1-2 until you can find every note without hesitation.
- **Listen for:** Clean tone on every note. No accidental string noise, no buzzing. At this stage, correct notes matter more than anything else.
- **Repeat:** Play through bars 1-2 at least 5 times slowly until the sequence feels memorized in your fingers.

### Step 2: Learn the Notes — Bars 3-4
Continue to bars 3-4 in the tab (first system, second half or second system).
- **Do this:** Same approach — note by note, no metronome. Bars 3-4 may introduce slightly different movement or a position shift. Take your time finding each note.
- **Listen for:** Clean transitions between notes, especially if there is a position shift. No gaps or stumbles.
- **Repeat:** 5 times through bars 3-4 until memorized.

### Step 3: Add the Bends
Now go back through bars 1-4 and identify every bend (marked in the tab with an upward arrow or "b" notation).
- **Do this:** For each bend, stop and practice it in isolation using the technique from Lesson 02: play the target pitch first, then bend to match. Do this 3 times per bend. Then play the phrase around the bend to hear it in context.
- **Listen for:** Each bend should hit its target pitch cleanly. A bend that is even slightly flat will make the whole phrase sound wrong. Be ruthless about accuracy here.
- **Repeat:** Isolate each bend until it feels reliable, then play bars 1-4 in sequence with the bends included.

### Step 4: Add Vibrato on Sustained Notes
Look through bars 1-4 for any note that is held for a beat or longer. These are your vibrato targets.
- **Do this:** On each sustained note, apply delayed-entry vibrato: hold the note clean for the first beat, then add wrist vibrato on beat 2 (or wherever feels natural in the phrase). If a sustained note is at the top of a bend, apply bend + vibrato from Lesson 02.
- **Listen for:** The vibrato should make each sustained note come alive — singing and expressive, not mechanical. The delayed entry should feel like the note "blooms."
- **Repeat:** Play bars 1-4 with vibrato added. Do this 5 times, focusing on making the vibrato feel natural, not forced.

### Step 5: Phrasing and Tempo
Now bring it together with a metronome. Start at 30-40% of the original solo tempo.
- **Do this:** Set your metronome to a slow tempo where you can play all 4 bars without stopping. If the solo is roughly 80-90bpm, start around 30-35bpm. Play through bars 1-4 with correct notes, bends, vibrato, and phrasing. Think vocally: where would a singer breathe? Let those spots have a tiny gap. Where would a singer lean into a note? Give those notes a bit more attack.
- **Listen for:** Musical flow. Even at slow tempo, the phrase should feel like it is going somewhere — not like a disconnected series of notes. The line should rise and fall naturally.
- **Repeat:** Play 3 times at your starting tempo. If clean, increase by 5bpm. Continue until you reach 50% tempo or until it starts falling apart — whichever comes first. If it falls apart, drop back 10%.

### Step 6: Consistency Pass
You need to be able to play bars 1-4 reliably, not just once.
- **Do this:** At 50% tempo (or whatever your max clean tempo is), play bars 1-4 three times in a row with no mistakes. A "mistake" is: wrong note, missed bend target, forgotten vibrato, or a stop/hesitation.
- **Listen for:** Consistency. Each pass should sound roughly the same. If one bar consistently breaks down, isolate that bar and loop it 10 times before trying the full 4-bar phrase again.
- **Repeat:** Until you can do 3 clean consecutive passes. If you cannot achieve this today, note which bar is the problem and focus there next session.

## Checkpoint
- [ ] Can you play bars 1-4 cleanly at 50% tempo (or close) with no wrong notes or hesitations?
- [ ] Do all bends hit their target pitches accurately?
- [ ] Are you applying vibrato on sustained notes with delayed entry?
- [ ] Does the phrasing feel vocal and natural — like a melody being sung, not notes being executed?

**All checked?** → Move to Lesson 04
**Not yet?** → Repeat this lesson next session. Focus on whichever checkpoint isn't passing.
