# Lesson 11: Section E — Bars 43-48 (Section E Complete)
**Phase:** 2 — Legato & Dynamics
**Prerequisites:** Lesson 10
**Estimated time:** 25 minutes
**You'll need:** Guitar, metronome, tuner

## Goal
Play the full Section E (bars 37-48) at 55% tempo and connect it with Section F so that you can play bars 37-56 (the entire back half of the solo) at 50% tempo.

## Context
This lesson completes Section E of the Man of Steel solo (D minor, standard tuning, ~56 bars). You already know bars 37-42 from Lesson 10 and bars 49-56 (Section F) from Phase 1. Bars 43-48 are the bridge between the solo's climax and its emotional resolution.

These bars complete the wind-down. Where bars 37-42 went from fire to release, bars 43-48 go from release to calm. The playing becomes melodic and spacious — fewer notes, more space, vocal-quality phrasing. This is similar in feel to Section A (the opening) but carries the emotional weight of everything that came before it. A listener who hears only bars 43-48 would feel resolution. A listener who heard the whole solo would feel catharsis.

Reference: See your tab.txt Section E or PDF pages 3-4.

## Warm-Up (5 min)
Play bars 37-42 from Lesson 10 twice through at 50% tempo with your metronome. Focus on the dynamic arc — peak intensity in bar 37, pulling back through bar 42. This serves as warm-up and review. If anything feels rough, spend a minute on it before moving on.

## Steps

### Step 1: Learn bars 43-44
- **Do this:** Open your tab for bars 43-44. These bars have fewer notes and more space than what you have been playing. Learn them note by note, slowly. Pay attention to note durations — some notes may be held for a full beat or more. Do not fill the space with extra notes or rush through rests.
- **Listen for:** Every note matters here. With fewer notes per bar, each one is exposed. Tone quality, vibrato on held notes, and precise intonation on any bends all become more critical. Play as if each note is a word in a sentence.
- **Repeat:** 5 times through slowly.

### Step 2: Learn bars 45-48
- **Do this:** Continue note by note through bars 45-48. This passage is very melodic — similar in character to Section A (the opening bars you learned in Phase 1) but played with the weight and emotion of everything that came before. If there are bends, they should be slow and deliberate. If there is vibrato, it should be warm, not aggressive.
- **Listen for:** A singing, vocal quality. Imagine a singer performing these notes — they would sustain, they would use dynamics within a single phrase, they would let some notes trail off naturally. Try to replicate that on the guitar.
- **Repeat:** 5 times through slowly.

### Step 3: Connect bars 43-48
- **Do this:** Play bars 43-48 as one flowing phrase. No metronome yet. The mood is calm, resolved, spacious. Let the music breathe. If there are rests or pauses in the tab, observe them fully — do not rush through silence.
- **Listen for:** Continuity of mood. These six bars should feel like one gentle exhale, not six separate events. The transition between bars should be seamless.
- **Repeat:** Until three clean passes without any hesitation.

### Step 4: Full Section E with metronome
- **Do this:** Set metronome to 40% tempo. Play all of Section E: bars 37-48. This is twelve bars covering the full emotional arc from climax to calm. The transition from bar 42 (end of Lesson 10 material) into bar 43 (new material) is the seam to watch — practice it in isolation if needed.
- **Listen for:** The complete dynamic arc across all twelve bars. Bar 37 is the loudest, most intense moment. Bar 48 should be the softest, most spacious moment. Everything in between is a gradual, controlled descent.
- **Repeat:** Increase by 5% increments: 40% → 45% → 50% → 55%. Three clean passes at each tempo before moving up.

### Step 5: Connect Section E to Section F (bars 37-56)
- **Do this:** Now play from bar 37 straight through to bar 56 — Section E into Section F. Set the metronome to 50% tempo. This is the entire back half of the solo. Section F (bars 49-56) is the material you learned in Phase 1 — the resolution, the ending.
- **Listen for:** The transition from bar 48 into bar 49. Section E winds down, and Section F completes the story. This should feel like landing after a long descent — arrival, resolution, peace. There should be no jarring shift in character between the two sections.
- **Repeat:** 3 times through at 50%.

### Step 6: Play bars 37-56 as a complete story
- **Do this:** One final performance of bars 37-56. Set the metronome to 50% tempo. Play it like a performance, not a practice run. Think about the emotional narrative: bars 37-38 are the peak, the most intense moment of the entire solo. Bars 39-48 are the gradual release, the long exhale. Bars 49-56 are the resolution, the final statement, peace.
- **Listen for:** Does it tell a story? Can you hear the arc from intensity to calm to resolution? If someone heard only these 20 bars, would they feel something? That is the standard.
- **Repeat:** Once, with full concentration and intention.

## Checkpoint

- [ ] Can you play Sections E + F connected (bars 37-56) at 50% tempo without stopping?
- [ ] Is the emotional arc from climax to resolution clear and audible across bars 37-56?
