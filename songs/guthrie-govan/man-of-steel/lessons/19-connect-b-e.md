# Lesson 19: Connect Sections B + E (Middle Sections)
**Phase:** 4 — Assembly
**Prerequisites:** Lesson 18
**Estimated time:** 30 minutes
**You'll need:** Guitar, metronome, tuner

## Context for the AI Guide
You are guiding Leo (intermediate guitarist) through a practice session on Guthrie Govan's "Man of Steel" solo. The solo is in D minor, standard tuning, approximately 56 bars long. Leo has completed Phases 1-3 (all 6 sections individually at 50-60% tempo) and Lesson 18 (Sections A and F connected at 65%). This lesson connects Sections B and E — the middle layers of the solo — and then builds the "outer shell" by linking A+B+E+F together. Section B (bars 9-16) begins building intensity after the opening. Section E (bars 37-48) winds down from the climax. Together with A and F, these four sections form the narrative frame of the solo.

## Goal
Play Section B (bars 9-16) and Section E (bars 37-48) each at 60% tempo cleanly, and play the "outer shell" of the solo (A+B+E+F, bars 1-16 then 37-56) at 55% with smooth transitions.

## Warm-Up (5 min)
**Legato patterns.** Without a metronome at first:
- On the high E string, play a hammer-on/pull-off sequence: 5h7p5h8p5 (index-ring-index-pinky-index). Do this on each string, moving down to the low E.
- Then set the metronome to a comfortable tempo (start around 70 BPM, sixteenth notes) and play legato runs across two strings in D minor: e.g., 5-7-8 on the B string, 5-7-8 on the high E, then back down.
- Focus on even volume between hammered and pulled notes. Every note should be audible and clean.

This matters because Sections B and E both rely on smooth legato phrasing.

## Steps

### Step 1: Section B (bars 9-16) at 55-60%
- **Do this:** Set metronome to 55% of original tempo. Play through Section B (bars 9-16) twice. This section starts building energy after the lyrical opening. Push to 60% if 55% is clean.
- **Listen for:** Increasing intensity compared to Section A. The phrasing should feel like it's gaining momentum. Legato lines should be smooth and even. Any position shifts should be seamless.
- **Repeat:** 3 times at your target tempo (55% or 60%).

### Step 2: Section E (bars 37-48) at 55-60%
- **Do this:** Same approach. Start at 55%, play through Section E (bars 37-48) twice. Push to 60%. Note: Section E is 12 bars (longer than most sections), so stamina matters here.
- **Listen for:** This section is winding down from the solo's climax (Sections C+D). It should feel like controlled descent — still intense but gradually releasing. The phrasing should breathe more as you approach bar 48.
- **Repeat:** 3 times at your target tempo.

### Step 3: Build the "outer shell" — A → B → E → F
- **Do this:** Set the metronome to 55%. Play the outer shell of the solo: Section A (bars 1-8) → Section B (bars 9-16) → skip ahead to Section E (bars 37-48) → Section F (bars 49-56). This is 36 bars — more than half the solo — and it's the narrative frame.
- **Listen for:** Does the intensity build through B? Does E feel like it peaks (even without the C+D fireworks before it) and begin to resolve? Does F feel like a peaceful conclusion? The dynamic arc should be: quiet/hopeful (A) → building (B) → winding down (E) → resolution (F).
- **Repeat:** 3 times. Focus on the overall shape of the music, not just individual notes.

### Step 4: Drill the transitions
- **Do this:** Isolate the transition points:
  - **Bar 8 → bar 9 (A into B):** Play bars 6-11 on loop. The shift from lyrical Section A into the more energetic Section B should feel natural, not jarring.
  - **Bar 48 → bar 49 (E into F):** Play bars 46-51 on loop. This is the moment where the solo shifts from winding down to final resolution. It should feel like a sigh of relief.
- **Listen for:** Seamless connections. No rhythmic hiccups, no tonal shifts that feel unintentional. The character changes between sections, but the music flows.
- **Repeat:** 5 times each transition, at 55%.

### Step 5: Run the outer shell 3 times at 55%
- **Do this:** Full outer shell (A+B+E+F) three times through at 55%. Each run should feel more natural than the last.
- **Listen for:** Improving flow with each repetition. By the third run, transitions should feel automatic.
- **Repeat:** Exactly 3 times. If the third run still has rough transitions, do 2 more.

### Step 6: Identify your weakest point
- **Do this:** After your runs, note the single weakest transition or passage. Write it down with the specific bar numbers. This becomes priority work for your next session.

## Checkpoint

- [ ] Can you play Section E (bars 37-48) cleanly at 60% tempo?
- [ ] Can you play the outer shell (A+B+E+F) at 55% without stopping?
- [ ] Are transitions between sections smooth (especially bar 8→9 and bar 48→49)?
