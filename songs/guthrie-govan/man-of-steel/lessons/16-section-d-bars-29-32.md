# Lesson 16: Section D — Bars 29-32
**Phase:** 3 — Speed
**Prerequisites:** Lesson 15 (Section D Bars 25-28)
**Estimated time:** 30 minutes
**You'll need:** Guitar, metronome, tuner

## Goal
Play bars 29-32 at 45% tempo and connect them with bars 25-28 for a continuous 8-bar passage at 40%.

## Context
Bars 29-32 continue Section D's sequence patterns. You may encounter wider string skips, more dynamic variation, and possibly sustained bends between fast passages that serve as brief breathing points. The main challenge here is stamina — maintaining picking-hand relaxation and accuracy over an increasingly long stretch of fast material.

**Reference:** See tab.txt Section D or the PDF (pages 2-3). Have the tab open during this lesson.

## Warm-Up (5 min)
Play bars 25-28 from Lesson 15 twice through at 45% tempo with metronome. This reviews the first half of Section D and gets your hands warm for the new material.

## Steps

### Step 1: Learn Bars 29-30 Slowly
- **Do this:** With the tab open, learn bars 29-30 note by note without a metronome. Look for any string-skipping patterns — the pick jumping over a string to reach a non-adjacent string. Also look for how these bars relate to bars 25-28 — is the sequence pattern continuing, evolving, or changing?
- **Listen for:** Clean fretting and accurate notes. If there are string skips, make sure you're not accidentally sounding the skipped string.
- **Repeat:** 5 times without metronome, then with metronome at 30%, increasing to 40%.

### Step 2: Learn Bars 31-32
- **Do this:** Learn bars 31-32 note by note. If there's a sustained bend between fast passages, treat it as a mini-break — but the bend must be accurate (check: does it bend a half step, whole step, or minor third?) and the vibrato must be controlled, not frantic.
- **Listen for:** Bend accuracy — play the target note as a fretted note first, then bend up to match it. The vibrato should oscillate evenly around the target pitch.
- **Repeat:** 5 times without metronome, then with metronome at 30%.

### Step 3: Connect Bars 29-32
- **Do this:** Play bars 29-32 continuously at 35% tempo with metronome.
- **Listen for:** Smooth transitions. Any sustained bend should flow naturally out of and back into the fast passages — no jarring change in character.
- **Repeat:** Increase to 40%, then 45%. Spend at least 2 minutes at each tempo.

### Step 4: Connect Bars 25-32 — The Full 8-Bar Block
- **Do this:** Play bars 25-32 continuously at 40% tempo with metronome. This is a big chunk of sequence material. Take a breath before starting and commit to playing through without stopping.
- **Listen for:** Consistency across all 8 bars. Common issue: starting strong but losing accuracy around bar 29-30 as fatigue sets in.
- **Repeat:** 3 passes at 40%. Rest 15 seconds between passes.

### Step 5: Stamina Check
- **Do this:** During your passes in Step 4, pay specific attention to the right hand. If it fatigues — hand tightening, pick grip getting harder, forearm burning — you are too tense. Stop immediately. Shake out your hand for 15 seconds. Reduce tempo by 5%. Try again with LESS grip pressure on the pick. The pick should be held just firmly enough not to fly out of your hand. Nothing more.
- **Listen for:** The difference in tone between a relaxed hand and a tense one. A tense hand produces a thinner, scratchier tone. A relaxed hand produces a fuller, more controlled sound.
- **Repeat:** If tension persists, spend 2-3 minutes playing the passage with an absurdly light pick grip to retrain the hand.

### Step 6: Three Clean Passes of Bars 25-32
- **Do this:** Play bars 25-32 three times at 40% tempo with metronome. These should be your best efforts of the session.
- **Listen for:** Accuracy, consistency, and relaxation across all three passes.
- **Repeat:** If you can't get 3 clean passes at 40%, drop to 35% and work there.

## Checkpoint
- [ ] Can you play bars 29-32 at 45% tempo?
- [ ] Can you play bars 25-32 connected at 40% tempo?
- [ ] Is the right hand NOT fatiguing during the 8-bar passage?
- [ ] Are any sustained bends accurate and vibrato controlled?
