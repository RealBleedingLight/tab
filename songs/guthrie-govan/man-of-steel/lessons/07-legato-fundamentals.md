# Lesson 07: Legato Fundamentals
**Phase:** 2 — Legato & Dynamics
**Prerequisites:** Lessons 01-06 (Phase 1 complete)
**Estimated time:** 25 minutes
**You'll need:** Guitar, metronome, tuner

## Goal
Play 4-note-per-string legato patterns across 3 strings evenly at 80bpm in 16th notes, with hammered and pulled-off notes matching the volume of picked notes.

## Warm-Up (5 min)
Chromatic hammer-on drill: On each string starting from the low E, pick fret 5 and hammer 6, 7, 8. Then reverse — pick fret 8, pull-off to 7, 6, 5. Go through all six strings. Keep it slow (60bpm, quarter notes). Focus entirely on making every note the same volume. Do not move on until each string sounds even.

## Concept
Legato means "smooth and connected." On guitar, this means using hammer-ons and pull-offs so that only the first note on each string is picked — the fretting hand produces the rest. The goal is seamless, flowing lines where you cannot tell which notes were picked and which were not.

This is central to Guthrie Govan's style. In Man of Steel, Sections B (bars 9-16) and E (bars 37-48) rely heavily on legato. If the hammered notes are weak or the pull-offs disappear, the phrases lose their singing quality.

Two common problems to watch for:
1. **Weak hammer-ons** — You need to strike the fret firmly with the fingertip, almost like the finger is a tiny hammer. Flabby, flat-finger contact will produce a ghost note.
2. **Silent pull-offs** — A pull-off is not just lifting the finger. You must flick the string slightly downward (toward the floor) as you release. This plucks the string with the fretting finger.

## Steps

### Step 1: Single-string ascending (high E)
- **Do this:** Pick fret 12 on the high E string, then hammer-on to 13, 14, 15. One pick stroke, three hammers. That is four notes total. Play them as even 16th notes — no rhythm, just focus on volume for now.
- **Listen for:** All four notes at equal volume. If notes 3 and 4 (ring and pinky fingers) are noticeably quieter, isolate them. Try hammering fret 14 and 15 repeatedly to build finger strength.
- **Repeat:** 10 times. Rest 10 seconds between reps.

### Step 2: Single-string descending (high E)
- **Do this:** Pick fret 15 on the high E string, then pull-off to 14, 13, 12. Remember: each pull-off needs a slight sideways/downward flick to sound the next note. Do not just lift the finger off.
- **Listen for:** Even volume across all four notes. The pull-off from 13 to 12 (middle finger to index) is usually the weakest — give it extra attention.
- **Repeat:** 10 times.

### Step 3: Repeat on B and G strings
- **Do this:** Run the same ascending pattern (pick 12, h13, h14, h15) and descending pattern (pick 15, p14, p13, p12) on the B string, then on the G string. Do 5 reps each direction on each string.
- **Listen for:** The lower/thicker strings require more force to hammer and pull cleanly. You will need to press harder on the G string than the high E. Adjust your effort per string.
- **Repeat:** 5 times ascending + 5 times descending on each string.

### Step 4: Cross-string ascending
- **Do this:** Chain the ascending pattern across three strings without stopping. High E string: pick 12, h13, h14, h15. Then B string: pick 12, h13, h14, h15. Then G string: pick 12, h13, h14, h15. Twelve notes total, three pick strokes.
- **Listen for:** The moment you transition from one string to the next — this is where legato falls apart for most players. The pick attack on the new string should not sound louder or accented compared to the hammered notes before it. Aim for one continuous stream of sound.
- **Repeat:** 10 times slowly, no metronome. Just get the transitions smooth.

### Step 5: Full pattern with metronome
- **Do this:** Play the 3-string pattern ascending AND descending (up across E-B-G, then back down G-B-E) with the metronome set to 60bpm, 16th notes. That is four notes per beat. Once you can do three clean passes at 60bpm, bump up to 65bpm. Continue in 5bpm increments. Target: 80bpm.
- **Listen for:** Rhythmic evenness — every note lands exactly on its 16th-note subdivision. Use the metronome to check. Also listen for any notes that drop out or buzz.
- **Repeat:** Spend 5-8 minutes here. If you stall at a tempo, stay there. Do not force it faster.

### Step 6: D natural minor legato pattern
- **Do this:** Now apply legato to scale tones from D natural minor instead of chromatic. On the high E string: pick fret 13, hammer 15, hammer 17. On the B string: pick 13, hammer 15, hammer 17. On the G string: pick 14, hammer 16, hammer 17. This is 3 notes per string (not 4), which changes the picking pattern and feel. These are the actual scale shapes Guthrie draws from in this solo.
- **Listen for:** The wider fret spacing (2-fret stretches instead of 1) makes the hammers harder. You may need to reposition your thumb behind the neck to reach. Each note should still ring clearly.
- **Repeat:** 5 times ascending, 5 times descending (pull-offs on the way down). Then try it with the metronome at 60bpm.

- [ ] Are your string transitions smooth — no loud pick accents breaking the legato flow?
