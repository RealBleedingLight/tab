# Lesson 26: Full Performance — Full Performance — Man Of Steel
**Phase:** Performance
**Prerequisites:** All previous lessons
**Estimated time:** 40 minutes
**You'll need:** Guitar, metronome, tuner, recording device (phone is fine)

## Goal
Play the complete solo from start to finish at 80% tempo (102 bpm) with musical expression, clean technique, and no stops.

## Warm-Up (10 min)
- 5 min technique warm-up: your weakest technique from this solo
- 5 min run through the section you're least confident on

## Performance Mindset
This is not a practice sessiois n — it's a performance. The difference matters:
- **No stopping.** If you hit a wrong note, keep going. A real performance doesn't have a rewind button.
- **Commit to every note.** Tentative playing sounds worse than a confident wrong note. Play with conviction.
- **Think in phrases, not notes.** You've learned the notes — now let the music flow through you. Think about where the melody is going, not where your fingers need to be.
- **Record yourself.** Listening back reveals things you can't hear while playing.

## Steps

### Step 1: Section Review
Play through each section once at 80% tempo as a quick refresher:
- Section A
- Section B
- Section C
- Section D
- Section E
- Section F
- Section G
- Section H
- Section I
- Section J
- Section K
- Section L
- Section M
- Section N
- Section O
- Section P
- **Do this:** One pass through each section. Don't stop to fix mistakes — just note which sections feel shakiest.
- **Listen for:** Overall flow and confidence level in each section.
- **Repeat:** Once through each section is enough. This is a warm-up, not practice.

### Step 2: Full Run-Through #1
Set your metronome to 102 bpm. Play the entire solo from bar 1 to the end. No stops.
- **Do this:** Start the metronome, take a breath, and play. If you stumble, keep the beat and jump back in at the next section if needed.
- **Listen for:** Overall musical arc — does the solo build energy naturally? Do the transitions feel connected?
- **Repeat:** Just once. After this run, take a 2-minute break. Note what went well and what broke down.

### Step 3: Spot-Fix the Weakest Point
From your first run-through, identify the single weakest moment — the transition or passage that felt most uncertain.
- **Do this:** Isolate that passage (2-4 bars) and loop it 10 times at 80% tempo. Then play the 4 bars before and after to practice entering and exiting that passage in context.
- **Listen for:** Clean, confident execution of the weak spot.
- **Repeat:** Until it feels solid, then move on.

### Step 4: Full Run-Through #2
Another complete performance. This one should be cleaner than the first.
- **Do this:** Full solo, no stops, metronome on. Record this one.
- **Listen for:** Improvement from run #1. Musical expression — are you playing with feeling or just executing notes?
- **Repeat:** Once. Listen back to the recording.

### Step 5: Full Run-Through #3 (Performance Standard)
Final run. This is the one that counts.
- **Do this:** Play the complete solo as if performing for an audience. Full commitment, full expression.
- **Listen for:** A complete, musical performance. Not perfect — musical. Does it sound like someone playing a solo, or someone practicing a solo?
- **Repeat:** Once. This is your benchmark. Record it and compare to the original.

## Checkpoint
- [ ] Can you play the complete solo without stopping?
- [ ] Are you at 80% tempo or higher?
- [ ] Does the recording sound musical (not just correct)?
- [ ] Are the transitions between sections smooth?

**All checked?** Congratulations — you've learned the solo! Continue practicing to push toward 100% tempo. Increase by 5 bpm per session.

**Not yet?** Note which sections need more work and revisit those specific lessons. Then come back to this performance lesson.
