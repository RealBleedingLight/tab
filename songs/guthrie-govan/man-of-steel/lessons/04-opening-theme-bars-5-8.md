# Lesson 04: Opening Theme — Bars 5-8
**Phase:** 1 — Foundation
**Prerequisites:** Lesson 03
**Estimated time:** 25 minutes
**You'll need:** Guitar, metronome, tuner

## Goal
Play bars 5-8 of the Man of Steel solo cleanly at 50% tempo and connect them to bars 1-4 for a complete Section A (bars 1-8) performance at 50% tempo.

## Warm-Up (5 min)
Play bars 1-4 from Lesson 03 twice through at whatever tempo you finished at last session. This serves as both warm-up and review. Pay attention to your bends and vibrato — are they still solid? If bars 1-4 feel rusty, spend a few extra minutes here before moving on. Your warm-up is done when bars 1-4 feel comfortable and musical.

## Concept: Pre-Bends and Dynamic Build
Bars 5-8 introduce two important elements:

**Pre-bends:** You learned the mechanics in Lesson 02, Step 6. In the actual solo, pre-bends appear around bars 5-6. The move is: silently bend the string to the target pitch before picking, then pick the already-bent note and release it downward. The audience hears a note that descends in pitch — the opposite of a normal bend. This creates a vocal "sigh" or "cry" effect that Guthrie uses for emotional emphasis.

**Dynamic build:** Bars 1-4 are the statement of the theme. Bars 5-8 are the response and development — they should feel like the intensity is gradually increasing. This does not mean playing louder suddenly; it means a subtle increase in pick attack, vibrato width, and rhythmic urgency. Think of a singer who starts a verse softly and leans in more as the verse progresses.

## Steps

### Step 1: Learn the Notes — Bars 5-6
Open your tab (tab.txt Section A or PDF page 1, systems 3-4). Read through bars 5-6.
- **Do this:** Play each note slowly, no metronome. Identify where the pre-bend occurs. When you reach it, practice the pre-bend in isolation: silently bend up, pick the bent note, release slowly. Do this 5 times before continuing.
- **Listen for:** Clean notes throughout. On the pre-bend, the first note you hear should be the upper target pitch, with a smooth descent. If it sounds like the pitch "scoops up" before descending, you picked before completing the silent bend.
- **Repeat:** 5 times through bars 5-6 until the notes and the pre-bend feel memorized.

### Step 2: Learn the Notes — Bars 7-8
Continue to bars 7-8. These bars begin to build intensity with some faster note groupings.
- **Do this:** Play through slowly. If there are groups of faster notes (sixteenths or triplets), isolate each group and play it note by note first, then gradually connect them. Do not try to play them at speed yet — just get the right notes in the right order.
- **Listen for:** Clean articulation on faster groupings. Each note should be distinct, not blurred together. If a group of notes feels awkward, check your fingering — there may be a more efficient way to finger the passage.
- **Repeat:** 5 times through bars 7-8. Spend extra time on any fast groupings.

### Step 3: Connect Bars 5-8
Now play bars 5-8 as a continuous phrase.
- **Do this:** Set your metronome to 40% tempo. Play through bars 5-8 without stopping. If you need to pause or stumble, note where it happened and loop that transition 5 times before trying the full 4-bar pass again.
- **Listen for:** Smooth transitions between bars. The phrase should flow — no gaps, no rushing, no stumbling at bar lines.
- **Repeat:** 3 clean passes at 40%. Then increase to 45%, then 50%.

### Step 4: Add Expression
Go back through bars 5-8 and add all the musical elements.
- **Do this:** Add bends (check pitch accuracy against target notes), vibrato on sustained notes (delayed entry), and the pre-bend from Step 1. Also think about dynamics: bars 7-8 should be slightly more intense than bars 5-6. You can achieve this with a slightly firmer pick attack and wider vibrato.
- **Listen for:** A phrase that builds. Bars 5-6 should feel like a continuation of bars 1-4, and bars 7-8 should feel like the energy is rising. The pre-bend should sound like an emotional "sigh."
- **Repeat:** 5 times through bars 5-8 at 50% tempo with full expression.

### Step 5: Connect Full Section A — Bars 1-8
This is the big moment: playing the complete opening section.
- **Do this:** Set your metronome to 40%. Play bars 1 through 8 without stopping. The transition from bar 4 to bar 5 is critical — practice this specific connection (last beat of bar 4 into the first beat of bar 5) in isolation if needed. Then increase tempo gradually toward 50%.
- **Listen for:** An 8-bar musical statement that feels like one complete thought. There should be a natural arc: bars 1-4 present the theme, bars 5-8 develop it with increasing intensity. The pre-bend in bars 5-6 should feel like a new color entering the phrase.
- **Repeat:** At each tempo, play through twice. Only increase tempo if both passes were clean.

### Step 6: Consistency Pass
Lock in Section A at 50% tempo.
- **Do this:** Play the full 8 bars (Section A) three times in a row at 50% tempo. No stops, no wrong notes, all bends accurate, vibrato on sustained notes, dynamic build audible.
- **Listen for:** Each of the three passes should sound musical and consistent. If a specific bar keeps breaking down, isolate it and loop 10 times, then return to the full section.
- **Repeat:** Until you achieve 3 clean consecutive passes. If you run out of time or focus, note your progress and continue next session.

## Checkpoint
- [ ] Can you play bars 5-8 cleanly at 50% tempo with accurate pre-bends and bends?
- [ ] Can you play the full Section A (bars 1-8) connected at 50% tempo without stops?
- [ ] Are pre-bends landing accurately — starting at the target pitch with a smooth descent?
- [ ] Is there an audible dynamic build from bar 1 through bar 8 — the section gets gradually more intense?

**All checked?** → Move to Lesson 05
**Not yet?** → Repeat this lesson next session. Focus on whichever checkpoint isn't passing.
