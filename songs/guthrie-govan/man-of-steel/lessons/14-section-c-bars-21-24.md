# Lesson 14: Section C — Bars 21-24 (Section C Complete)
**Phase:** 3 — Speed
**Prerequisites:** Lesson 13 (Section C Bars 17-20)
**Estimated time:** 30 minutes
**You'll need:** Guitar, metronome, tuner

## Goal
Play the full Section C (bars 17-24) at 50% tempo with no wrong notes and consistent rhythm.

## Context
Bars 21-24 complete Section C. They continue the chromatic and scalar intensity of bars 17-20 with multi-string runs and possibly descending sequences. After this lesson, you'll have all 8 bars of Section C — the first big shred block of the solo — under your fingers.

**Reference:** See tab.txt Section C or the PDF (page 2). Have the tab open during this lesson.

## Warm-Up (5 min)
Play bars 17-20 from Lesson 13 twice through at 45% tempo with metronome. This serves as both warm-up and review. If bars 17-20 feel rough, spend an extra few minutes cleaning them up before moving on.

## Steps

### Step 1: Learn Bars 21-22 Slowly
- **Do this:** With the tab open, learn bars 21-22 note by note without a metronome. As before, identify repeating patterns — many fast passages are built from 4-6 note cells that shift position up or down the neck.
- **Listen for:** Clean fretting, no buzzing. Correct notes. Notice whether the passage ascends, descends, or does both.
- **Repeat:** 5 times through without metronome until memorized. Then add metronome at 30% and increase to 40%.

### Step 2: Learn Bars 23-24
- **Do this:** Learn bars 23-24 note by note. These bars transition toward Section D — the character may shift slightly from pure chromatic runs to more pattern-based or sequence-based material. Pay attention to how bar 24 ends, because it needs to flow into bar 25 (Section D) later.
- **Listen for:** Any change in feel from the preceding bars. Identify what makes these bars different from 17-22.
- **Repeat:** 5 times without metronome, then with metronome at 30%, increasing to 40%.

### Step 3: Connect Bars 21-24
- **Do this:** Play bars 21-24 continuously at 35% tempo with metronome. Focus on smooth transitions between bars — no pauses at bar lines.
- **Listen for:** Even rhythm, clean notes, relaxed picking hand.
- **Repeat:** Increase to 40%, then 45%. Spend at least 2 minutes at each tempo.

### Step 4: Full Section C — Bars 17-24
- **Do this:** Play all 8 bars of Section C (17-24) at 40% tempo with metronome. This is a significant chunk of fast material. It is normal to feel fatigue — if your right hand tenses, stop, shake out, reduce tempo by 5%.
- **Listen for:** Consistency from bar 17 all the way through bar 24. The most common problem is starting strong and losing accuracy by bar 22-23 due to fatigue or loss of focus.
- **Repeat:** 3 passes at 40%.

### Step 5: Push to 50%
- **Do this:** Attempt bars 17-24 at 45%, then 50% tempo. If 50% is too fast today, that is fine — note your max clean tempo and target 50% in a future session.
- **Listen for:** Clean notes and even rhythm. If specific bars crumble, mark them for isolated practice.
- **Repeat:** 2-3 attempts at your max clean tempo.

### Step 6: Section B into Section C
- **Do this:** Connect Section B (bars 9-16, legato material from Phase 2) with Section C (bars 17-24). Start at 40% tempo. The transition at bar 17 should feel like shifting gears — from smooth legato to aggressive picked runs.
- **Listen for:** The gear shift at bar 17. The tonal character changes from legato smoothness to percussive picking. Make sure the transition doesn't cause a rhythmic stumble.
- **Repeat:** 2-3 passes at 40%. Note how the transition feels.

## Checkpoint
- [ ] Can you play the full Section C (bars 17-24) at 50% tempo?
- [ ] Are there zero wrong notes in the chromatic passages?
- [ ] Can you connect Sections B and C (bars 9-24) at 45% tempo?
- [ ] Does your stamina hold through all 8 bars of Section C?

**All checked?** → Move to Lesson 15
**Not yet?** → Repeat this lesson next session. Focus on the weakest bars within Section C.
