# Lesson 15: Section D — Bars 25-28
**Phase:** 3 — Speed
**Prerequisites:** Lesson 12 (Alternate Picking Drill)
**Estimated time:** 30 minutes
**You'll need:** Guitar, metronome, tuner

## Goal
Play bars 25-28 at 45% tempo with consistent sequence patterns, clean string crossings, and accurate position shifts.

## Context
Section D (bars 25-36) features sequenced patterns — repetitive picking and/or legato figures that shift position across the neck. Where Section C was about chromatic intensity, Section D is about CONSISTENCY: playing the same pattern cleanly as it moves up, down, or across strings. You may also encounter hybrid picking (pick + fingers) and string-crossing passages. This lesson covers the first 4 bars.

**Reference:** See tab.txt Section D or the PDF (page 2). Have the tab open during this lesson.

## Warm-Up (5 min)
Three-string alternate picking from Lesson 12: high E (12-13-14-15), B (12-13-14-15), G (12-13-14-15), ascending and descending. 70-80bpm, 16th notes. 5 minutes to loosen up the picking hand.

## Steps

### Step 1: Learn Bars 25-26 — Find the Pattern
- **Do this:** With the tab open, learn bars 25-26 note by note without a metronome. The key task: identify the repeating cell or pattern. Section D sequences are often built from a 4-6 note figure that shifts up or down the neck. Once you find the pattern, learn it ONCE, then just move it to each new position.
- **Listen for:** The moment the pattern "clicks" — when you see it as one shape moving rather than dozens of individual notes.
- **Repeat:** Play through 5 times without metronome. Then add metronome at 30%.

### Step 2: Practice the Position Shifts
- **Do this:** The position shift between repetitions of the pattern is where mistakes happen. Isolate just the LAST note of one pattern repetition and the FIRST note of the next. Practice this 2-note transition slowly, 10 times in a row.
- **Listen for:** No gap between pattern repetitions. The shift should be inaudible — same rhythm and volume as every other note.
- **Repeat:** Do this for every position shift in bars 25-26.

### Step 3: Learn Bars 27-28
- **Do this:** Learn bars 27-28 note by note. These bars may introduce string-crossing — the pattern moves to adjacent strings rather than just shifting position on the same string. This requires precise pick control.
- **Listen for:** When crossing strings, both strings should ring at equal volume. The pick shouldn't accidentally mute the previous string or hit an adjacent open string.
- **Repeat:** 5 times without metronome, then with metronome at 30%.

### Step 4: Hybrid Picking Check
- **Do this:** Check whether bars 25-28 contain any passages that benefit from hybrid picking (using the pick + middle or ring finger). Signs: a note on a lower string followed immediately by a note on a non-adjacent higher string. If present, practice isolated: hold pick normally, pluck the higher string with your middle finger. Practice alternating: pick on one string, finger pluck on another, back and forth.
- **Listen for:** The finger-plucked note should match the volume and tone of the picked note. No snapping or popping unless the tab indicates it.
- **Repeat:** 2 minutes of isolated hybrid picking practice if applicable. Skip this step if no hybrid picking is needed.

### Step 5: Connect Bars 25-28
- **Do this:** Play bars 25-28 continuously at 35% tempo with metronome. Focus on the transitions between bars and between pattern repetitions.
- **Listen for:** Consistency — every repetition of the pattern should sound identical in rhythm, volume, and articulation.
- **Repeat:** Increase to 40%, then 45%. Spend at least 2 minutes at each tempo.

### Step 6: Three Clean Passes
- **Do this:** Play bars 25-28 three times at 45% tempo with metronome. Shake out hands between passes.
- **Listen for:** All three passes should sound the same. If pass 3 is worse than pass 1, fatigue or tension is creeping in — address it by relaxing grip pressure.
- **Repeat:** If 45% isn't clean for 3 passes, drop to 40%.

## Checkpoint
- [ ] Can you play bars 25-28 at 45% tempo?
- [ ] Are the sequence patterns consistent — same rhythm and volume on each repetition?
- [ ] Are string crossings clean with no muted or extra notes?
- [ ] Are position shifts smooth with no rhythmic gaps?

**All checked?** → Move to Lesson 16
**Not yet?** → Repeat this lesson next session. Isolate the specific transitions that break down.
