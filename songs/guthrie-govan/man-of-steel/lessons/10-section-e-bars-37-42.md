# Lesson 10: Section E — Bars 37-42
**Phase:** 2 — Legato & Dynamics
**Prerequisites:** Phase 1 complete (Lessons 01-06)
**Estimated time:** 25 minutes
**You'll need:** Guitar, metronome, tuner

## Goal
Play bars 37-42 of Man of Steel at 50% tempo with controlled bends, sustained vibrato on held notes, and a clear dynamic arc from peak intensity down to something more restrained.

## Context
This lesson jumps to Section E of the solo (D minor, standard tuning, ~56 bars). You do not need to have completed Lessons 08-09 first — this section is learned in parallel because it connects directly to Section F (bars 49-56), which you already know from Phase 1.

Section E is the emotional climax of the solo. Bars 37-42 represent the peak — the most intense, dramatic moment — followed by the beginning of a release. Big sustained bends with vibrato, fast runs that give way to space, and raw expressive power. This is where the solo says everything it has been building toward, and then starts to let go.

Think of this section as exhaling after holding your breath.

Reference: See your tab.txt Section E or PDF page 3.

## Warm-Up (5 min)
Bending exercises from Lesson 02: On the B string, fret 15, practice full-step bends (B up to C#) and 1.5-step bends (B up to D). Hold each bend at pitch for 4 beats, then add vibrato while holding the bend. Do 5 reps of each. This section demands rock-solid bending and vibrato — get those fingers ready.

## Steps

### Step 1: Learn bars 37-38 slowly
- **Do this:** Open your tab for bars 37-38. These bars likely contain some of the fastest, most intense playing in the entire solo — this is the climax. Go note by note, extremely slowly. If there are fast legato runs, isolate them: take just 4-6 notes at a time and loop them until the fingering feels natural.
- **Listen for:** Even at very slow speed, the intensity should come through. These are not delicate notes — play them with conviction. Every hammer-on and pull-off needs to speak clearly.
- **Repeat:** Work through bars 37-38 in small chunks until you can play them end to end without stopping. This may take 5-8 minutes.

### Step 2: Learn bars 39-40
- **Do this:** Bars 39-40 begin the release. The intensity starts to ease. You will likely encounter sustained bends — long, full-step bends held with vibrato, possibly 1.5-step bends. There may also be slower, more deliberate melodic phrases. Learn note by note.
- **Listen for:** The contrast with bars 37-38. These bars should feel like a sigh of relief after the intensity. The sustained bends need to hit their target pitch precisely (use your tuner to check if needed) and the vibrato should be controlled, not frantic.
- **Repeat:** 5 times through slowly.

### Step 3: Learn bars 41-42
- **Do this:** Continue note by note. Bars 41-42 have more sustained notes and space. The solo is breathing again. Fewer notes per bar, but each one carries more emotional weight.
- **Listen for:** Let notes ring for their full written duration. Do not rush to the next note. If a note is held for two beats, hold it for two full beats. The silence and sustain are as important as the notes themselves.
- **Repeat:** 5 times through slowly.

### Step 4: Connect bars 37-42 with dynamic arc
- **Do this:** Play all six bars in sequence. No metronome yet. The critical thing to get right is the DYNAMIC ARC: bars 37-38 are at maximum intensity (loud, aggressive, fast). Bars 39-40 pull back to about 70% intensity. Bars 41-42 settle to about 50%. You are gradually turning the volume and urgency down across these six bars.
- **Listen for:** A clear, audible difference in intensity between bar 37 and bar 42. If someone walked into the room during bar 37, they should feel energy and fire. If they walked in during bar 42, they should feel the tension releasing.
- **Repeat:** 3 times, focusing entirely on the dynamic shape. Do not worry about perfection in the notes — focus on the arc.

### Step 5: Sustained bends with vibrato
- **Do this:** Go back and find every sustained bend in bars 37-42. Practice each one in isolation. Bend to pitch, hold for the full written duration, and add vibrato. The vibrato should start after the bend reaches its target — do not add vibrato while still bending up.
- **Listen for:** Pitch accuracy (is the bend reaching the right note?), sustain (does the note ring for the full duration or die early?), and vibrato quality (even, controlled oscillation — not a nervous wobble).
- **Repeat:** Each bend 5 times in isolation.

### Step 6: Bring up to tempo with metronome
- **Do this:** Set metronome to 40% tempo. Play bars 37-42. Work up to 50% in 5% increments. Three clean passes at each tempo before moving up. "Clean" means correct notes, correct rhythm, and the dynamic arc from Step 4 is present.
- **Listen for:** The fast passages in bars 37-38 are the bottleneck. If you cannot play them cleanly at a given tempo, isolate just those beats and drill them at that tempo before attempting the full 6-bar passage again.
- **Repeat:** Spend 5-8 minutes working from 40% to 50%.

## Checkpoint
- [ ] Can you play bars 37-42 cleanly at 50% tempo?
- [ ] Are sustained bends held for their full duration with controlled vibrato at correct pitch?
- [ ] Is the dynamic arc audible — clearly louder/more intense at bar 37, clearly pulling back by bar 42?
