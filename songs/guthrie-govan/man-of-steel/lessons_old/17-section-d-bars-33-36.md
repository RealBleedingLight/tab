# Lesson 17: Section D — Bars 33-36 (Section D Complete)
**Phase:** 3 — Speed
**Prerequisites:** Lesson 16 (Section D Bars 29-32)
**Estimated time:** 30 minutes
**You'll need:** Guitar, metronome, tuner

## Goal
Play the full Section D (bars 25-36) at 50% tempo and connect Sections C + D (bars 17-36) at 45% tempo.

## Context
Bars 33-36 close Section D and transition toward Section E — the climax of the solo. The patterns may become more intense, building momentum as they approach the peak. After this lesson, you'll have Sections C and D complete — the entire fast middle section of Man of Steel — and Phase 3 will be finished.

**Reference:** See tab.txt Section D or the PDF (pages 2-3). Have the tab open during this lesson.

## Warm-Up (5 min)
Play bars 25-32 from Lessons 15-16 once through at 40% tempo with metronome. This reviews the first 8 bars of Section D and warms up your hands for the final 4 bars.

## Steps

### Step 1: Learn Bars 33-34 Slowly
- **Do this:** With the tab open, learn bars 33-34 note by note without a metronome. As always, look for the repeating pattern or cell. Notice whether the intensity is increasing compared to earlier bars — Guthrie often builds toward a climax through increasing density or wider intervals.
- **Listen for:** Clean notes, correct fingering. Identify any new technical demands that weren't present in bars 25-32.
- **Repeat:** 5 times without metronome, then with metronome at 30%, increasing to 40%.

### Step 2: Learn Bars 35-36 — The Transition Point
- **Do this:** Learn bars 35-36 note by note. Pay special attention to how bar 36 ends — this is the handoff to Section E (the climax, which you learned in Phase 2). There may be a build in intensity, a specific melodic gesture, or a shift in rhythmic character that prepares for the climax.
- **Listen for:** How the musical energy of bar 36 sets up bar 37. Even though you're playing slowly, try to feel the momentum building.
- **Repeat:** 5 times without metronome, then with metronome at 30%.

### Step 3: Connect Bars 33-36
- **Do this:** Play bars 33-36 continuously at 35% tempo with metronome.
- **Listen for:** Smooth flow through all 4 bars with no rhythmic stumbles at bar lines.
- **Repeat:** Increase to 40%, then 45%. Spend at least 2 minutes at each tempo.

### Step 4: Full Section D — Bars 25-36
- **Do this:** Play all 12 bars of Section D (bars 25-36) at 40% tempo with metronome. This is a significant stamina challenge. Before starting, consciously relax your shoulders, arms, and hands.
- **Listen for:** Consistency from bar 25 through bar 36. The sequence patterns should sound even and controlled throughout. No loss of accuracy in the final bars.
- **Repeat:** Push to 45%, then 50% if possible. If 50% isn't clean today, note your max clean tempo. 3 passes at your max clean tempo.

### Step 5: Sections C + D — Bars 17-36
- **Do this:** Play bars 17-36 continuously at 40% tempo with metronome. This is the entire fast middle section of the solo — 20 bars of demanding material. Start with a fresh attempt (shake out hands, take a breath).
- **Listen for:** The transition from Section C to Section D around bar 24-25. Section C is chromatic/scalar; Section D is pattern-based/sequential. The character should shift but the rhythm should remain steady.
- **Repeat:** Increase to 45% if possible. Even one clean pass at 45% is a significant achievement.

### Step 6: Identify Remaining Weak Spots
- **Do this:** Play bars 17-36 three times at your max clean tempo. After each pass, mentally note which specific bars still break down. Write down the bar numbers.
- **Listen for:** Recurring problem spots — if the same bar breaks every time, that bar needs isolated practice in future maintenance sessions.
- **Repeat:** This is diagnostic, not performance. Be honest about what's clean and what isn't.

## Checkpoint
- [ ] Can you play the full Section D (bars 25-36) at 50% tempo?
- [ ] Can you connect Sections C + D (bars 17-36) at 45% tempo?
- [ ] Does your stamina hold for the full 20-bar stretch without right-hand fatigue?
- [ ] Is the transition from Section C to Section D smooth?

**All checked?** → Phase 3 complete. Move to Lesson 18 (Phase 4: Assembly).
**Not yet?** → Repeat this lesson next session. Target the specific bars that break down.
