# Lesson 06: Outro — Bars 53-56 (Section F Complete)
**Phase:** 1 — Foundation
**Prerequisites:** Lesson 05
**Estimated time:** 20 minutes
**You'll need:** Guitar, metronome, tuner

## Goal
Play the complete Section F (bars 49-56) at 50% tempo and review Section A (bars 1-8) at 60% tempo, with consistent vibrato and emotional connection between both sections.

## Warm-Up (5 min)
Play through bars 49-52 from Lesson 05 twice at 50% tempo. This is both warm-up and review. Focus on wide vibrato and full note durations. If it does not feel solid, spend a few extra minutes here. Then play one pass of bars 1-4 from the opening theme to wake up that muscle memory. Your warm-up is done when both passages feel comfortable.

## Steps

### Step 1: Learn the Notes — Bars 53-56
Open your tab (tab.txt Section F or PDF page 4, final systems). Read through bars 53-56.
- **Do this:** Play each note slowly, no metronome. These are the final bars of the solo — the notes are very sustained with minimal movement. The solo fades out here. Identify every note and its duration. The final note(s) should ring out and decay to silence.
- **Listen for:** Clean, clear tone. These notes are completely exposed — there is nowhere to hide. Any imperfection in intonation or fretting will be obvious.
- **Repeat:** 5 times through until memorized.

### Step 2: Connect to Bars 49-52
Now play the full Section F: bars 49 through 56.
- **Do this:** Set your metronome to 40% tempo. Play from bar 49 through bar 56 without stopping. Pay special attention to the transition from bar 52 into bar 53 — practice this join in isolation if it feels awkward. The final notes of bar 55-56 should ring out and fade naturally. Do not cut them short.
- **Listen for:** A continuous musical statement that gradually settles and fades. The energy should decrease from bar 49 to bar 56 — the solo is winding down and coming to rest. The very last note should feel like a period at the end of a sentence.
- **Repeat:** 3 times at 40%. If clean, move to 45%, then 50%.

### Step 3: Full Section F at 50% — Three Clean Passes
Lock in the complete outro.
- **Do this:** Play bars 49-56 three times in a row at 50% tempo. Apply everything from Lesson 05: wide vibrato, full durations, decaying dynamics, and the feeling of resolution. On the final note, let it ring until it naturally fades. Do not mute it.
- **Listen for:** Consistency across all three passes. Each one should sound musical and complete. The vibrato should be wide and even throughout. The dynamic shape should feel like a long exhale that ends in stillness.
- **Repeat:** Until 3 clean passes are achieved. If a specific bar or transition keeps breaking, isolate and loop it.

### Step 4: Review Section A at 60%
Now go back to the opening theme. It has been at least one or two sessions since you worked on it — let us see if it has improved.
- **Do this:** Set your metronome to 50% tempo and play bars 1-8 (full Section A). If it feels comfortable at 50%, push to 55%, then 60%. Apply everything: accurate bends, delayed vibrato, pre-bends in bars 5-6, dynamic build from bar 1 to bar 8.
- **Listen for:** Improvement. Does it feel easier than it did in Lesson 04? Muscle memory and ear training from the intervening lessons should make the opening feel more natural. If 60% feels clean, great. If not, find your current ceiling and note it.
- **Repeat:** 3 passes at your maximum clean tempo. Target is 60%, but any improvement over your Lesson 04 tempo is progress.

### Step 5: The Emotional Arc — Section A to Section F
You now know the beginning and the end of the solo. Play them back to back to feel the emotional journey.
- **Do this:** Play Section A (bars 1-8) at 50% tempo. Then pause for a few seconds — imagine the intense middle sections happening (the fast runs, the climax, the tension). Then play Section F (bars 49-56) at 50% tempo. Feel the contrast: the opening is hopeful and lyrical; the outro is reflective and resolved.
- **Listen for:** An emotional difference between the two sections. Section A should feel like the beginning of a story — fresh, expectant. Section F should feel like the ending — peaceful, settled. If both sections sound the same emotionally, experiment with dynamics: make Section A slightly brighter in tone and Section F slightly warmer and softer.
- **Repeat:** 2-3 times through the full arc. This is not about perfection — it is about feeling the shape of the solo as a whole, even though you only know two pieces of it so far.

## Checkpoint
- [ ] Can you play the full Section F (bars 49-56) at 50% tempo with wide vibrato, full durations, and a fading dynamic shape?
- [ ] Can you play Section A (bars 1-8) at 60% tempo with accurate bends, vibrato, and dynamic build?
- [ ] Do both sections feel emotionally connected — Section A hopeful, Section F resolved — when played back to back?
- [ ] Is your vibrato consistent and controlled throughout both sections?
