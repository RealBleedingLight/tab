# Lesson 20: Connect Sections C + D (Fast Core)
**Phase:** 4 — Assembly
**Prerequisites:** Lesson 19
**Estimated time:** 30 minutes
**You'll need:** Guitar, metronome, tuner

## Context for the AI Guide
You are guiding Leo (intermediate guitarist) through a practice session on Guthrie Govan's "Man of Steel" solo. The solo is in D minor, standard tuning, approximately 56 bars long. Leo has completed Phases 1-3, Lesson 18 (A+F at 65%), and Lesson 19 (B+E at 60%, outer shell at 55%). This lesson tackles the hardest part: connecting Sections C and D — the fast, technically demanding core of the solo (bars 17-36). These sections contain the most challenging alternate picking, legato runs, and position shifts. The tempo will likely be limited by these sections, and that's expected. After connecting C+D, Leo will attempt the full middle stretch (B through E).

## Goal
Play Sections C+D (bars 17-36) connected at 50% tempo cleanly, and play the full middle of the solo (bars 9-48) at 40% without stopping.

## Warm-Up (5 min)
**Alternate picking drill.** Set the metronome to your current max clean alternate picking tempo (whatever that is — be honest):
- Pick a single-string chromatic pattern: 5-6-7-8 on the high E string, strict alternate picking (down-up-down-up). Sixteenth notes.
- Move this pattern to each string. Focus on consistent pick attack and minimal excess motion.
- Then do a cross-string pattern: 5-6-7-8 on the B string, then 5-6-7-8 on the high E string, maintaining strict alternate picking across the string change.
- 2 minutes on single-string, 3 minutes on cross-string patterns.

This matters because Sections C and D are where the solo's technical demands peak.

## Steps

### Step 1: Section C (bars 17-24) — Check and rebuild
- **Do this:** Set metronome to 45% of original tempo. Play through Section C (bars 17-24). Has it degraded since Phase 3? Be honest. If notes are sloppy or timing is off, drop the tempo until it's clean, then build back up to 50%.
- **Listen for:** Clean alternate picking, accurate position shifts, even note volume. Every note should be deliberate — no "faking it" through fast passages by blurring notes together.
- **Repeat:** 3 times at 50% (or at whatever tempo is your current clean max for this section).

### Step 2: Section D (bars 25-36) — Check and rebuild
- **Do this:** Same approach. Start at 45%, play Section D (bars 25-36). Check for degradation. Rebuild to 50% if needed. Note: Section D is 12 bars, so it requires more stamina than C.
- **Listen for:** Same criteria as Step 1. Additionally, watch for tension creeping into your picking hand or fretting hand. If you're gripping harder as the section goes on, you're fighting the guitar. Relax.
- **Repeat:** 3 times at 50% (or current clean max).

### Step 3: Connect C → D with transition focus
- **Do this:** The transition at bar 24 → bar 25 is critical. Isolate bars 23-26 (the last 2 bars of C and the first 2 bars of D). Set the metronome to 45%. Play just these 4 bars on loop.
- **Listen for:** Smooth handoff between the two sections. No rhythmic stumble at the bar 24-25 boundary. The intensity should continue flowing — C and D are one continuous arc of energy, not two separate pieces glued together.
- **Repeat:** 10 times. Yes, ten. This transition is worth the investment.

### Step 4: Run bars 17-36 (C+D complete)
- **Do this:** Set metronome to 45%. Play through all of bars 17-36 (Sections C and D together). Three times through. Then push to 50%.
- **Listen for:** Continuity. Does it feel like one 20-bar passage or two sections stapled together? The energy should build through C and peak somewhere in D. Watch for stamina — your picking hand may fatigue by bar 30+.
- **Repeat:** 3 times at 45%, then 3 times at 50% if 45% is solid.

### Step 5: The big connection — B → C → D → E (bars 9-48)
- **Do this:** This is the entire middle of the solo — 40 bars. Set the metronome to 40%. Play from bar 9 (start of Section B) through bar 48 (end of Section E) without stopping. The tempo will be limited by whichever section is hardest (probably C or D). That's OK. Play the whole stretch at whatever tempo C and D can handle.
- **Listen for:** The narrative arc: B builds momentum, C ignites, D peaks, E winds down. Even at 40% tempo, this shape should be audible. If everything sounds the same intensity, you're missing the dynamics.
- **Repeat:** Twice. This is demanding — two full runs is enough.

### Step 6: Identify the weakest 4 bars
- **Do this:** After your runs through bars 9-48, pinpoint the weakest 4 consecutive bars. Write down the bar numbers. These become your priority drill material for the next session and the warm-up for Lesson 21.

## Checkpoint
- [ ] Can you play Sections C+D (bars 17-36) connected at 50% tempo?
- [ ] Can you play the full middle (bars 9-48) at 40% without stopping?
- [ ] Does your stamina hold through the entire 40-bar stretch?
- [ ] Are the transitions B→C (bar 16→17) and D→E (bar 36→37) smooth?

**All checked?** → Move to Lesson 21
**Not yet?** → Repeat this lesson next session. Spend extra time on the weakest 4 bars you identified in Step 6.
