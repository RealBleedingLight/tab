# Lesson 18: Connect Sections A + F (Melodic Bookends)
**Phase:** 4 — Assembly
**Prerequisites:** Phases 1-3 complete (all 6 sections individually at 50-60% tempo)
**Estimated time:** 25 minutes
**You'll need:** Guitar, metronome, tuner

## Context for the AI Guide
You are guiding Leo (intermediate guitarist) through a practice session on Guthrie Govan's "Man of Steel" solo. The solo is in D minor, standard tuning, approximately 56 bars long. Leo has already learned all 6 sections (A through F) individually at 50-60% tempo in Phases 1-3. This lesson begins Phase 4 (Assembly), where sections get connected together. Section A (bars 1-8) and Section F (bars 49-56) are the melodic bookends of the solo — they share a similar lyrical, vocal quality. A opens the solo with hope; F closes it with resolution.

## Goal
Play Section A (bars 1-8) and Section F (bars 49-56) each at 65% tempo cleanly, with a clear emotional arc connecting them (A = hopeful opening, F = peaceful resolution).

## Warm-Up (5 min)
**Vibrato and bending review.** Set your metronome to 60 BPM. On the B string:
- Play a full-step bend at the 10th fret (D up to E). Hold the bend and add vibrato. Sustain for 4 beats. Release slowly.
- Repeat at the 13th fret (F up to G), same approach.
- Do 5 reps at each position. Focus on: hitting the target pitch cleanly, vibrato that's wide and even (not nervous wobble), and smooth release.
- Switch to the G string, 12th fret. Same drill.

This matters because Sections A and F are full of expressive bends and vibrato — they're the soul of the solo.

## Steps

### Step 1: Section A (bars 1-8) — Rebuild to 65%
- **Do this:** Set metronome to 60% of the original tempo. Play through Section A (bars 1-8) twice. This should feel comfortable since you mastered it in Phase 3. Now bump the metronome up to 65%.
- **Listen for:** Clean note articulation, bends hitting their target pitches, vibrato that sounds intentional (not shaky). The phrasing should breathe — don't rush through the spaces between phrases.
- **Repeat:** 3 times at 65%. If it's not clean, drop back to 60% and do 3 more reps before trying 65% again.

### Step 2: Section F (bars 49-56) — Rebuild to 65%
- **Do this:** Same approach. Start at 60%, play through Section F (bars 49-56) twice. Push to 65%.
- **Listen for:** Section F should sound like it carries weight — this is the end of the solo. The notes might be similar in character to Section A, but the delivery should feel like arriving home after a long journey. Vibrato should be wider and more confident here.
- **Repeat:** 3 times at 65%. Same rule — drop back if it's not clean.

### Step 3: Play A then F back to back
- **Do this:** Set the metronome to 60%. Play Section A (bars 1-8), then immediately jump to Section F (bars 49-56). Yes, you're skipping the entire middle of the solo. That's the point.
- **Listen for:** Emotional continuity. Section A should sound like a question — open, hopeful, setting out. Section F should sound like the answer — resolved, peaceful, having been through something. They're the same character, but F carries the weight of the whole solo. If both sections sound the same emotionally, you're missing the arc.
- **Repeat:** 3 times. Each time, try to make the contrast between A and F more distinct.

### Step 4: Record and listen back
- **Do this:** Record yourself playing A then F (at 60-65%). Use your phone, a DAW, whatever is handy. Then listen back with fresh ears.
- **Listen for:** Does A sound like a question and F like an answer? Is your vibrato quality consistent throughout? Are bends accurate? Does it sound musical or mechanical?
- **Repeat:** If the recording reveals problems, address the specific issue and record again.

### Step 5: Push to 70% (if ready)
- **Do this:** If 65% felt clean and comfortable on both sections, bump the metronome to 70%. Run each section twice, then play them back to back.
- **Listen for:** Any degradation in tone, timing, or expression. Speed is worthless without feel.
- **Repeat:** Only push this if 65% was genuinely solid. There's no rush.

### Step 6: Log your tempos
- **Do this:** Write down your current clean max tempo for Section A and Section F separately. "Clean" means: correct notes, good timing, expressive bends and vibrato, no stumbles.
- This becomes your baseline for future sessions.

- [ ] When you play A then F back to back, is there a clear emotional arc (hope → resolution)?
- [ ] Is your vibrato quality consistent and controlled throughout both sections?
