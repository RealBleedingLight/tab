# Lesson 02: Bending Accuracy
**Phase:** 1 — Foundation
**Prerequisites:** Lesson 01
**Estimated time:** 25 minutes
**You'll need:** Guitar, metronome, tuner

## Goal
Accurately bend to target pitch (full step and 1.5 steps) on the B string and apply controlled vibrato at the top of the bend — hitting the target pitch at least 8/10 times for full bends and 6/10 for 1.5 step bends.

## Warm-Up (5 min)
Start with 2 minutes of the vibrato exercise from Lesson 01: 3rd finger on B string fret 15, delayed vibrato entry on beat 2, at 60bpm. This warms up your wrist and reinforces the motion you will need at the top of your bends. Then spend 3 minutes doing slow bends on the G string fret 12 — just push the string up gently and release, feeling the resistance. Do not worry about pitch yet. Just get the muscles warm and the finger calluses engaged.

## Concept: Bending Technique
String bending is how guitarists mimic the human voice — sliding between pitches rather than jumping between frets. In the Man of Steel solo, Guthrie uses bends constantly, especially full step and 1.5 step bends on the B and high E strings.

- **Use multiple fingers.** When bending with your 3rd (ring) finger, tuck your 1st and 2nd fingers behind it on the same string. All three fingers push together. This gives you strength and control.
- **The push comes from the wrist and forearm**, not just the fingers. Think of it as rotating your forearm to push the string upward (toward the ceiling).
- **Bends go UP in pitch by pushing the string UP** (toward the low E string / ceiling). On the B and high E strings, you always push up.
- **A full step bend = 2 frets of pitch.** Bending B string fret 15 up a full step produces the same pitch as fret 17.
- **A 1.5 step bend = 3 frets of pitch.** Bending B string fret 15 up 1.5 steps produces the same pitch as fret 18. This requires significantly more force.
- **Intonation matters more than anything.** A bend that misses the target pitch by even a quarter tone sounds terrible. Train your ear ruthlessly.

## Steps

### Step 1: Learn the Target Pitch — Full Bend
You need to know exactly what pitch you are bending TO before you bend.
- **Do this:** Play B string fret 17 (the note A). Pick it 5 times and let it ring. Memorize this pitch. Sing or hum it. Now play B string fret 15 (the note G) without bending — hear how different it is. The full bend must bridge that exact gap.
- **Listen for:** The specific pitch of fret 17. Burn it into your ear. You should be able to hear it in your head before you play it.
- **Repeat:** Play fret 17, then fret 15 unbent, back and forth 5 times. Feel the interval.

### Step 2: Full Step Bend — With Reference
Now bend fret 15 up to match fret 17. Use your 3rd finger with 1st and 2nd fingers supporting behind it.
- **Do this:** Play fret 17 (target). Immediately bend fret 15 up and try to match the pitch. Hold the bend and compare. Are they the same? If not, adjust. Then release.
- **Listen for:** When the bend matches the target, you will hear a "lock" — the pitch stops sounding tense or off. If you are flat, you will hear a slight sourness. If you are sharp (less common), ease off slightly.
- **Repeat:** 10 times. Each time: play target first, then bend. Keep score — how many of 10 are spot on?

### Step 3: Full Step Bend — Without Reference
Now try the bend cold, without playing the target pitch first.
- **Do this:** Pick B string fret 15 and bend up to what you think is the full step. Hold it. Then (while still holding the bend) pick fret 17 on another string or release and check. Were you right?
- **Listen for:** Trust your ear from Step 2. The pitch should feel "settled" and in tune when you hit the target.
- **Repeat:** 10 times. If you hit fewer than 8/10, go back to Step 2 for another round of 10 with the reference pitch.

### Step 4: 1.5 Step Bend
The target is now B string fret 18 (the note Bb/A#) — 3 frets above fret 15.
- **Do this:** Play fret 18 five times to memorize the target. Then bend fret 15 up to match. This requires noticeably more force than the full bend. Make sure all three fingers are engaged and the power comes from your wrist/forearm rotation.
- **Listen for:** Same "lock" feeling as the full bend, but the pitch is higher. This bend is physically demanding — if your hand fatigues, rest for 30 seconds between attempts.
- **Repeat:** 10 times with reference, then 10 without. Target: 6/10 accurate without reference.

### Step 5: Bend + Vibrato at the Top
This is a signature Guthrie Govan technique used extensively in the Man of Steel solo. You bend to the target pitch and then add vibrato WHILE HOLDING THE BEND.
- **Do this:** Bend B string fret 15 up a full step (to match fret 17). Once you reach the target pitch, HOLD IT THERE and add vibrato. The vibrato oscillates the pitch slightly above and below the BENT pitch — not the original unbent pitch. Your wrist is doing two jobs: maintaining the bend pressure AND adding the vibrato rotation.
- **Listen for:** The bent pitch should remain centered on the target (fret 17 equivalent) with the vibrato wavering around it. If the vibrato causes you to lose the bend (pitch drops), you need more forearm support to hold the bend while the wrist adds vibrato.
- **Repeat:** 10 times. Sustain the bend + vibrato for at least 3 beats at 60bpm. This is physically tiring — rest between attempts if needed.

### Step 6: Pre-Bends
A pre-bend means you silently bend the string UP to the target pitch BEFORE you pick it, then pick the already-bent note and release it down to the unbent pitch. This creates a descending pitch effect. It appears in bars 5-6 of the solo.
- **Do this:** Silently bend B string fret 15 up a full step (no picking). Make sure you have reached the target pitch — you can check by picking while bent and comparing to fret 17. Once confident, do the full move: silently bend up, pick the bent note, then slowly release the bend so the pitch slides down to the unbent fret 15 pitch.
- **Listen for:** The first sound you hear should be the TARGET pitch (fret 17 equivalent), then a smooth descent to fret 15. If the initial pitch is flat, you did not pre-bend far enough.
- **Repeat:** 10 times. The silent pre-bend should become instinctive — you need to "know" when you have reached the right pitch without hearing it first.

## Checkpoint
- [ ] Can you hit the full step bend target pitch (B string fret 15 → fret 17 equivalent) at least 8 out of 10 times without a reference?
- [ ] Can you hit the 1.5 step bend target pitch (B string fret 15 → fret 18 equivalent) at least 6 out of 10 times?
- [ ] Can you hold a full step bend at the target pitch and add vibrato for at least 3 beats at 60bpm?

**All checked?** → Move to Lesson 03
**Not yet?** → Repeat this lesson next session. Focus on whichever checkpoint isn't passing.
