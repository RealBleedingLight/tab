# Man of Steel — Theory & Scale Analysis
## Guthrie Govan Solo

### Key and Tonality
- The Man of Steel theme is primarily in **D minor** (with the relative major F)
- Guthrie's solo works within this framework but takes some characteristic liberties

### Scales Used

**1. D Natural Minor (D Aeolian)**
- D E F G A Bb C
- The foundation scale for most of the solo
- The opening melodic theme (Section A) stays largely within this scale
- This is the "safe" scale — where the solo feels grounded and emotional

**2. D Harmonic Minor**
- D E F G A Bb C#
- The raised 7th (C#) creates tension and a classical/neoclassical flavor
- Guthrie uses this sparingly for dramatic effect, especially in faster runs
- The C# → D resolution is a powerful melodic tool

**3. D Dorian**
- D E F G A B C
- The raised 6th (B natural instead of Bb) gives a slightly brighter, jazzier color
- Guthrie slips into Dorian on some of the legato passages — characteristic of his jazz fusion background

**4. Chromatic Passages**
- Sections C and D include chromatic connecting notes that don't belong to any single scale
- These are approach notes — chromatic notes that resolve to scale tones
- This is a hallmark of Guthrie's style: blending scalar and chromatic playing so fluidly you can't tell where one ends and the other begins

### Modal Approach
Guthrie doesn't rigidly stick to one scale. He treats the chord changes as opportunities to shift color:
- Over Dm: D Aeolian or D Dorian
- Over Bb: Bb Lydian flavor (the #4 creates brightness)
- Over Am or A: sometimes implies A Phrygian Dominant for a darker edge

This is **modal interchange** — borrowing from parallel modes to create different emotional colors over the same progression.

### Phrasing Concepts

**Call and Response:** Many phrases in sections A and B follow a "question and answer" structure — a phrase ascends (question), then a second phrase descends or resolves (answer).

**Tension and Release:** The solo's arc is one giant tension-release curve:
- Low tension: Sections A, F (melodic, spacious)
- Building: Sections B, E
- Peak tension: Sections C, D (dense, fast, chromatic)

**Rhythmic Variety:** Guthrie mixes:
- Sustained notes with vibrato (creates space)
- Sextuplet runs (creates urgency)
- Triplet phrases (creates swing/groove)
- 32nd notes (creates intensity)

### Why This Solo Works
The Man of Steel solo isn't just technical — it tells a story. It starts intimate and vulnerable (Superman's humanity), builds through struggle and intensity (the conflict), peaks with explosive power (the hero emerges), and resolves with emotion (hope). Understanding this narrative helps you play it with the right feel, not just the right notes.
