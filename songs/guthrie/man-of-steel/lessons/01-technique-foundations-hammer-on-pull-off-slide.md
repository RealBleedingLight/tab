# Lesson 01: Technique Foundations — Hammer-On & Pull-Off & Slide
**Phase:** Foundation
**Prerequisites:** None
**Estimated time:** 20 minutes
**You'll need:** Guitar, metronome, tuner

## Goal
Build a solid foundation in the techniques needed for this solo: Hammer-On, Pull-Off, Slide.

## Warm-Up (5 min)
- Warm up with basic hammer-on exercises on the B string around fret 12
- Warm up with basic pull-off exercises on the B string around fret 12
- Warm up with basic slide exercises on the B string around fret 12

## Hammer-On
Sound a higher note by forcefully fretting it with a finger without picking — the fretting hand generates the note

Pick the first note, then firmly and quickly bring another finger down on a higher fret on the same string. The 'hammering' finger must land precisely on the fret with enough force to sustain the note. Keep the original finger down.

### Common Mistakes
- Not hammering hard enough — the note dies or is too quiet
- Landing between frets instead of right behind the target fret
- Lifting the first finger when hammering with the second
- Hammering too slowly — the motion should be quick and snappy

### Variants to Practice
- **Single hammer-on** (notation: h)
- **Hammer-on from nowhere** (notation: h)

## Pull-Off
Sound a lower note by plucking the string with the fretting finger as it lifts off

Fret both notes simultaneously. Pick the higher note, then pull the higher finger slightly downward (toward the floor) as you lift it off, plucking the string with the fingertip. The lower finger should already be in position. The pull-off is not just lifting the finger — you must flick the string.

### Common Mistakes
- Simply lifting the finger instead of pulling/flicking the string
- Not having the lower note already fretted before the pull-off
- Pulling too far and hitting adjacent strings
- Insufficient volume on the pulled-off note

### Variants to Practice
- **Single pull-off** (notation: p)
- **Pull-off to open string** (notation: p)

## Slide
Smoothly glide from one fret to another while maintaining pressure on the string

Pick the first note, then slide your finger along the string to the target fret while maintaining constant pressure. Don't lift the finger at all during the slide. The key is consistent pressure — too light and the note dies, too heavy and you slow down.

### Common Mistakes
- Lifting finger pressure during the slide, causing the note to die
- Overshooting or undershooting the target fret
- Pressing too hard, making the slide sluggish
- Not picking the initial note loudly enough — the slide needs energy to sustain

### Variants to Practice
- **Slide up** (notation: /)
- **Slide down** (notation: \)
- **Slide into (from below)** (notation: /)
- **Slide out (to nowhere)** (notation: /)


## Steps

### Step 1: Practice Hammer-On
Pick the first note, then firmly and quickly bring another finger down on a higher fret on the same string. The 'hammering' finger must land precisely on the fret with enough force to sustain the note. Keep the original finger down.
- **Do this:** Start slow and focus on clean execution. Practice hammer-on on the B string, frets 12-15.
- **Listen for:** Clean, controlled hammer-on with consistent tone.
- **Repeat:** 10 repetitions, then move to the next technique.

### Step 2: Practice Pull-Off
Fret both notes simultaneously. Pick the higher note, then pull the higher finger slightly downward (toward the floor) as you lift it off, plucking the string with the fingertip. The lower finger should already be in position. The pull-off is not just lifting the finger — you must flick the string.
- **Do this:** Start slow and focus on clean execution. Practice pull-off on the B string, frets 12-15.
- **Listen for:** Clean, controlled pull-off with consistent tone.
- **Repeat:** 10 repetitions, then move to the next technique.

### Step 3: Practice Slide
Pick the first note, then slide your finger along the string to the target fret while maintaining constant pressure. Don't lift the finger at all during the slide. The key is consistent pressure — too light and the note dies, too heavy and you slow down.
- **Do this:** Start slow and focus on clean execution. Practice slide on the B string, frets 12-15.
- **Listen for:** Clean, controlled slide with consistent tone.
- **Repeat:** 10 repetitions, then move to the next technique.
